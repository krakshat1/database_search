Cancer is amongst the most fatal diseases causing 9.6 million deaths and an estimated 18.1 million new cases of cancer globally in 2018 (Ferlayet al, 2018). Given the mortality rate it is of prime importance for scientific community to develop anti cancer vaccines and drugs. But unfortunately onco-researchers have got limited success in developing anti cancer therapeutics/drugs/vaccines because of problems like cross-reactivity, tolerance and insufficient immune response (Igneyet al, 2002). Also, every cancerous tumor is heterogeneous having highly distinct set of mutations with limited fraction shared between diverse patients (Sahinet al, 2018). These hurdles led oncologist to tailor/develop/design personalized anticancer delivery system/medicine-a medical strategy which works by targeting the unique genetic and pathological characteristics of individual patient (Mandal et al, 2016).
